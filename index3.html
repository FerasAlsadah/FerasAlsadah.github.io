Rules of the game

Football is one of the oldest sports in the world and one of the most recognized, there are many tournaments and powerful and competitive football leagues. What comes from that would be strict rules and regulations.

Each game must include one referee and two assistant referees (Lineman). It is the referee's job to act as a timekeeper, make decisions on fouls, free kicks, throw-ins, penalties, etc. and add them on time at the end of each half. The referee may consult the assistant referee about the decision at any time during the match. It is the assistant referee's job to find offside in the match, throw in to either team and assist the referee in all decision-making processes as needed.

Offside can be called when an attacking player is in front of the last defender when the pass is played through to them. The offside area is designed to discourage players from simply hanging around the opponent’s goal waiting for a pass. To be onside they must be placed behind the last defender when the ball is played to them. If the player is in front of that last defender then he is deemed to be offside and free kick to the defending team will be called. A player cannot be caught offside in their own half. The goalkeeper does not count as a defender. If the ball is played backwards and the player is in front of the last defender then he is deemed to be not offside.
