League system

Every nation (with a few exceptions) has its own league system, with many levels going from the elite to the grassroots. For example, the English Premier League is the Premier League, followed by the Championship, League One and League Two. Below, England is broken down into smaller geographic parts with their respective leagues.

At the end of each season, the worst team in each league will be downgraded by one level, and the best team in each league will be upgraded by one level. For example, three Premier League teams will be replaced by three championship teams.

The best teams in Europe qualify for the prestigious Champions League. Because there is a difference in quality between the major leagues, some countries get more places in the Champions League - the allocation is determined by a five-year factor calculated by the performance of each league's clubs in European competitions. Do well, and your league will get more Champions League slots. and if you perform poorly, your league may lose those slots
